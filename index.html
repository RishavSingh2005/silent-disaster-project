Silent Disaster
Flood Risk  ·  Early Signal System  ·  India  v1.0

Risk awareness
before a disaster
becomes one.

A transparent, rule-based environmental monitoring system that surfaces early flood risk signals for Indian regions — before conditions become visible as damage.

Why this matters

Most flood-related decisions happen after conditions are already critical. Drainage fails, evacuation routes submerge, and emergency resources are deployed reactively. This system is designed to shift that window earlier — not to predict, but to flag when environmental signals are trending toward risk.

What this is not

This is not machine learning. There is no black box. Every score is produced by transparent, auditable rules based on rainfall trends, cumulative volume, historical sensitivity, and temperature interaction. The full logic is readable in the Data Transparency panel.

Regions covered

Eight Indian cities with distinct flood risk profiles — from the Brahmaputra basin in Assam to the semi-arid plateau of Rajasthan. Each region is assessed over a 7-day or 14-day environmental observation window.
